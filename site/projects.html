Our Projects

These are the experiments Prospero is willing to discuss in public—the sanctioned glimpses into the systems we are bending, optimizing, or quietly reinventing. Behind each codename sits a vault of research memos, late-night breakthroughs, and operational rituals designed to give our circle an advantage while everyone else is distracted by the noise.

Project Feedsnip

The average person donates roughly sixty hours every month to bottomless social feeds designed to keep them hungry. Feedsnip severs that loop. We cloak addictive timelines across major platforms, forcing algorithms into silence while we harvest the useful signal for later review.

Subscribers receive distilled summaries they can tune like a precision instrument—dial up the competitive intelligence, mute the outrage, surface only the threads that matter. Feedsnip doesn’t just save time; it hands back agency in an era where attention is a leased asset.

Our beta cohorts are reporting entire weeks liberated each quarter. Imagine redirecting that reclaimed bandwidth into building, learning, or simply breathing. That is the promise.

In phase two we’re layering in predictive prompts, identifying what you should monitor before it trends, and recommending replacement habits that keep your creative muscles active. The feed loses its fangs; you gain a compass.

Darknet Navigator

The internet you know is a storefront window. Behind it sprawls a labyrinthine marketplace—unindexed, unregulated, and overflowing with knowledge most people are conditioned to ignore. Darknet Navigator is our blueprint for moving through that space with intent instead of fear.

Adam has been mapping hidden networks since childhood, learning which gates to unlock, which vendors to trust, and which forums will evaporate if you pronounce their names incorrectly. Navigator compresses that education into a disciplined guide: how to harden your operational security, where to locate legitimate discounts worth thousands each month, and what signals reveal genuinely rare intelligence.

We dig deep into the culture, too—how to read the etiquette of clandestine markets, when to disappear, and how to trade value without leaving fingerprints. This isn’t for thrill seekers; it’s for strategic operators who understand the difference between curiosity and recklessness.

This is not a mass product. Only a limited run will be released at a premium price, ensuring the tactics stay sharp and the community remains composed of people who understand the responsibility that comes with access.

Data Brokerage

Large language models are already converging on the same answers because they eat the same public datasets. The only way to build a sharper edge is with contrarian inputs—clean, proprietary data streams no one else can touch. Prospero is constructing a brokerage that sources, sanitizes, and packages those datasets for clients who want to outperform the herd.

We are engineering ingest pipelines, compliance filters, and monetization frameworks that respect privacy while amplifying capability. When the market realizes their models are plateauing, our partners will have access to the raw ore required to train systems that feel clairvoyant.

Each dataset is vetted through adversarial testing, ensuring the intelligence we deliver cannot be easily replicated or reverse-engineered. We’re forging relationships with niche experts who guard obscure archives, and we compensate them fairly so the supply remains steady.

Progress updates will surface as milestones are met. Until then, assume we are busy in the field pulling signals from the noise.

Upstart Startup

Most startup documentaries are glossy postmortems. Upstart Startup will be the opposite: a low-budget, brutally honest chronicle of Prospero as it iterates in real time. Expect whiteboard scribbles, late-night pivots, brand experiments that flop before they fly, and the humor required to stay sane while building in public.

We are currently assembling the visual language—design systems, narrative beats, and the tone that says “yes, we’re serious, but we’re also here to enjoy the chaos.” When the series drops, it will serve as both a teaching tool and a reminder that momentum is forged through relentless, imperfect action.

Viewers will gain access to the messy middle: the hiring decisions we wrestle with, the investor conversations that change strategy in a single sentence, and the experiments that fail so fast they barely make it to sunrise. Transparency becomes our marketing.

More transmissions will arrive as episodes take shape. Consider this your invitation to watch the myth being made, not just the final cut.

Streamdust

The Internet was once a frontier. A place of curiosity, chaos, and creativity where anyone could share an idea, a tool, or a story. It wasn’t optimized for clicks. It was a living, breathing archive of human imagination — and it was free. But somewhere along the way, the open web was buried under layers of consumerism.

Beneath all of that noise, the gems remain. There are pockets of knowledge, creativity, and sheer delight still thriving in the shadows. Essays, archives, games, and experiments that remind us of what the Internet used to be — raw, real, and free.

Streamdust exists to uncover that hidden world.

We’re here to dig through the digital dust and surface what matters: the rare, the overlooked, the forgotten, the timeless. We do this not with algorithms, but with curation and community.
